About TAO Association of Nepal

Preserving ancient wisdom for modern times

Our Journey

Founded in 1995, the TAO Association of Nepal has been dedicated to preserving and sharing the ancient wisdom of Taoism in the heart of the Himalayas. We believe that the principles of harmony, balance, and natural living are as relevant today as they were thousands of years ago.

Our History

1995 - Foundation

TAO Association of Nepal was established by a group of dedicated practitioners seeking to bring Taoist teachings to Nepal.

2000 - First Temple

Construction completed on our first meditation center in Kathmandu, providing a sacred space for practice.

2005 - National Recognition

Officially registered as a non-profit spiritual organization with the Government of Nepal.

2010 - Branch Expansion

Opened centers in Pokhara, Chitwan, and Lalitpur to serve growing communities across Nepal.

2020 - International Connections

Established partnerships with Taoist organizations in China, Thailand, and India for cultural exchange.

2025 - Temple Construction

Launched ambitious project to build our largest temple complex in Kathmandu.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

  • • Preserve and propagate authentic Taoist teachings in Nepal
  • • Provide accessible spiritual education and practice opportunities
  • • Foster a community of practitioners dedicated to personal growth
  • • Build and maintain sacred spaces for meditation and study
  • • Promote harmony between humanity and nature

Our Vision

  • • Become Nepal's premier center for Taoist learning and practice
  • • Establish a network of temples across all major cities
  • • Create international exchange programs with Asian Taoist centers
  • • Integrate Taoist principles into modern wellness practices
  • • Inspire a generation to live in harmony with the Tao

Our Founder

Master Surya Bahadur Thapa

Founder & Chief Spiritual Guide

Master Surya Bahadur Thapa dedicated his life to the study and practice of Taoist philosophy after a transformative journey to China in 1985. Trained under renowned Taoist masters at Wudang Mountains, he returned to Nepal with a vision to share these ancient teachings with his homeland.

With over 40 years of practice and 30 years of teaching experience, Master Thapa has guided thousands of students on their spiritual journey. His teachings emphasize the practical application of Taoist principles in modern life, making ancient wisdom accessible to contemporary practitioners.

"The Tao is not a destination but a way of walking. Every step taken with awareness is a step towards harmony with the universe."
